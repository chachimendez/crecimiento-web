From March 5 to 28, 2025, the Aleph Hub hosted Aleph March '25, Crecimiento's third pop-up city. More than 3,000 people took part and 141 speakers came through. The focus was on startups. Rayuela, the competition track, selected 49 out of 88 applicants and accompanied them for six weeks with mentorship, workshops and investor meetings. The program closed with a Demo Day where 15 startups presented, and BuFi, Ensuro and Bondi took the win.

[video](https://youtu.be/Yihv6wzOYqE?list=PL0Jb2_9xzReLkckruji2lpbywBzfLp6F_)

The programming, meanwhile, was organized by tracks. The first AI Day took place, with talks on autonomous agents, onchain music and gaming. The Interop Summit gathered, for the first time in Argentina, the teams working on Ethereum interoperability. Regulation Day ran its third edition with the participation of directors from the CNV, the UIF and the IGJ, along with legal teams from Pomelo, Lemon, Ripio and Bitso. Finally, a two-day crypto conference featured an online keynote by Vitalik Buterin and a fireside chat with Daniel Rabinovich, COO of Mercado Libre.

![Tim Draper during the opening of Aleph March '25](/images/blog/aleph-march-25-3.jpg)

The Aleph Hackathon ran its third edition from March 21 to 23 in a hybrid format: more than 260 participants, 206 active hackers, 61 projects submitted and more than 40 thousand dollars in prizes.

![The Aleph Hub auditorium during a talk](/images/blog/aleph-march-25-1.jpg)

The Crecimiento Support Program activated its first initiatives within the pop-up: University Day and Cátedra Blockchain alongside universities and student communities, the ShipBA hackathon for AI-powered applications, and a women in crypto gathering on March 8.
